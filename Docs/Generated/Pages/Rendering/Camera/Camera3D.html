class Camera3D extends Matrix4 implements Camera
Represents the camera in a 3D scene.
Properties
direction Vector3
The direction the camera is facing. This must be a unit vector, and starts as (0, 0, 1)
stable
right Vector3
The local right direction of the camera, in the XZ World-Space plane
immutable
up Vector3
The local up direction of the camera, in World-Space
immutable
screen Frustum
A small World-Space Frustum containing the frusta of all the camera's lenses. This only updates when cacheScreen() is called
immutable
lenses Lens[]
A list of the Lenses of the camera, defining how and what objects are projected to different parts of the screen. This must contain at least one Lens
Methods
get lensLens
Returns the first Lens of the camera. Equivalent to .lenses[0];
set transform(transform)
Sets the current pose of the camera to a given Transform3D, aligning local X, Y, and Z to their corresponding Camera-Space axes.
Parameters
transform Transform3D
The new pose for the camera
get transformTransform3D
Retrieves the current pose of the camera as a Transform3D.
cacheScreen()Frustum
Synchronizes .screen to match the location and orientation of the camera and returns it. This also calls .cacheScreen() on all the camera's Lenses.
advance(amount)void
Moves the camera by a specified amount along its direction.
Parameters
amount Number
The amount to move forward (in World-Space)
strafe(amount)void
Moves the camera in the X-Z World-Space plane perpendicular to its direction.
Parameters
amount Number
The amount to strafe (in World-Space). Positive values will move right, and negative values will move left
lift(amount)void
Moves the camera along the y axis by a specified amount.
Parameters
amount Number
The amount to move by. Positive values will move down, and negative values will move up
look(xAngle, yAngle)void
Points the camera in a specific direction, with a specified angle from +z on the horizontal and vertical axes. The vertical input is clamped to avoid gimbal lock.
Parameters
xAngle Number
The angle in radians about the y axis from +z to the camera direction
yAngle Number
The angle in radians about the x axis from +z to the camera direction
